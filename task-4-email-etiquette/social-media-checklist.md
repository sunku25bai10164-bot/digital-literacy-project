Social Media Checklist: Responsible Use for College Students
Do's (Recommended Practices)
Do Review Your Privacy Settings. Take time to thoroughly understand and configure the privacy settings on all your social media platforms to control who can see your content and who can tag you.

Do Pause and Reflect Before Posting. Always take a few seconds to consider the potential long-term consequences, tone, and impact of a post on your personal and professional reputation before hitting "submit."

Do Treat Everyone with Respect. Practice empathy and courteous communication in all your social media interactions, treating online discussions with the same respect you would in face-to-face conversations.

Do Use a Professional Profile Picture and Bio. On professional platforms like LinkedIn, and even on personal platforms if your profile is semi-public, ensure your profile picture and bio present you in a positive, approachable, and competent light.

Do Verify Information Before Sharing. Help prevent the spread of misinformation by taking a moment to fact-check articles, news, or claims before you share them with your network.

Don'ts (Practices to Avoid)
Don't Over-Share Sensitive Personal Information. Avoid posting confidential details like your home address, phone number, specific locations in real-time, or financial information to protect your safety and identity.

Don't Post Inappropriate Content. Refrain from sharing photos, videos, or comments involving illegal activities, excessive partying, derogatory language, discriminatory remarks, or nudity.

Don't Vent About Academic or Professional Frustrations. Avoid posting negative complaints about professors, classmates, coursework, or internships, as this can be perceived negatively by current or future employers.

Don't Engage in Cyberbullying or Harassment. Never participate in online hate speech, personal attacks, trolling, or the intentional humiliation of others.

Don't Ignore How Your Online Activity Reflects on You. Remember that social media creates a permanent digital footprint that can be easily accessed by future employers, graduate schools, and other professional networks.
